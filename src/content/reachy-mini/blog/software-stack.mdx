Reachy Mini turns one. [Reachy Mini](https://huggingface.co/blog/reachy-mini)
is a small **open-source robot**: a head that moves, two antennas, a microphone, a
speaker and a camera. It fits on a desk. You can talk to it, it can look at you,
and it can react. To mark its first birthday, we are releasing **the whole
ecosystem** that has grown around it over the past year.

The robot is only half the story. The other half is the galaxy around it: a
**smartphone app** to set it up and talk to it, a **store** full of things it can
do, and the **Hugging Face ecosystem** to back it all.

This article walks through those pieces one by one, in the order you meet them as
a new owner - the robot, the app you use it with, the galaxy of apps it opens up,
and the way to make your own:

## Using your robot

### The mobile app

The main way to use Reachy Mini is the mobile app. It is what sets up a
brand-new robot in the first place - finding it over Bluetooth and putting it
on your Wi-Fi - and once the robot is online, you sign in with your Hugging
Face account, your robot shows up in a list, and you tap to connect.
Everything you do day to day starts here.

<StoreBadges card appStore="https://apps.apple.com/app/id6766823749" googlePlay="https://play.google.com/store/apps/details?id=com.pollenrobotics.reachymini" />

### One account, all your robots

For your robot to show up in that list, it has to be signed into the **same
Hugging Face account** as the app. That shared account is how the app knows
which robots are yours - on your desk, at the office, or on the other side of
the world.

<HtmlEmbed
  src="stack-one-account.html"
  title="One account ties it all together"
  caption="Your Hugging Face account is the pivot: the app and every one of your robots sign into it, so any robot on that account shows up as yours - wherever it happens to be."
/>

Behind the scenes, it all goes through one small server in the middle. When your
robot comes online, it announces itself there as one of yours; the app, signed
into the same account, just asks that server which robots are yours - and that
is the list you tap from.

Tapping one opens a direct connection between the app and the robot: the server
introduces the two, then steps aside while the video, the sound and the movement
flow straight between them. That works the same whether the robot is on your
Wi-Fi or across an ocean, so near and far feel identical - same app, same tap.

<Note variant="success" emoji="🌍" title="One app, local or remote">
The app never has to know where the robot is. Drive one on your desk over local
Wi-Fi, or one in another country over the internet, with the exact same app and
the exact same tap.
</Note>

We are also working on a fully-local mode that finds and connects to a robot on
your own network, with no account and no central server at all.

### First contact, over Bluetooth

The very first thing to do with a brand-new robot is get it onto your Wi-Fi.
We first shipped this as a desktop app, and we learned a lot from it - but a
desktop app is one more thing to install, and its connection was never quite
dependable enough. So we moved the whole first-contact flow onto **Bluetooth**,
straight from the phone: the app discovers the robot over Bluetooth, you pick
your network and hand over the password, and the robot joins your Wi-Fi. No
cable, no desktop install - and it is far more reliable.

### The first wake-up

The very first time you connect, the app does not drop you straight into a
conversation. It plays a short guided wake-up: the robot starts "still asleep"
and you bring it to life together, one step at a time. Along the way it quietly
checks that every part works - it hears you through the microphone, moves its
head and antennas, looks at you through the camera, and speaks through the
speaker - and you give it a name. It ends with a little greeting, and from then
on you are straight into talking to it.

<Note variant="neutral" emoji="✨" title="Once, and only once">
The wake-up is a one-time welcome, remembered per robot. After that, connecting
takes you right where you left off - no wizard, no setup, just your robot.
</Note>

<Video src="/videos/wake-up.mp4" aspect="1002 / 1720" maxWidth={300}>The first wake-up: the app walks you through waking the robot and meeting it - checking its microphone, movement, camera and speaker, then naming it.</Video>

### Conversation is built in

The headline feature is talking to the robot. For now the conversation runs in
the app, which drives the robot as you talk. Start it and you are in a real
back-and-forth voice conversation: you speak, the robot listens, thinks, and
answers out loud while its head and antennas move along. It can also glance
through its camera, play an emotion, and remember things you told it earlier.

<Stack layout="2-column" gap="large">
<Video src="/videos/conversation.mp4" aspect="1004 / 1730" maxWidth={320}>Talking to Reachy Mini from the mobile app: hold a real voice conversation, and give it any personality you like from a single line.</Video>
</Stack>

Giving it a personality is just as easy: describe a vibe in one sentence and out
comes a name, a voice and a whole character - or, if nothing comes to mind, hit
surprise-me and the app invents an original one for you. A few personas ship
built in, and the ones you make are saved and swappable.

### Drive it, and add to it

Beyond conversation, the app lets you watch the camera feed and steer the head
with an on-screen joystick, like a small remote-controlled companion. And it is
your door to the app store: browse new apps and launch them straight onto the
robot, no install (more on that later).

### Lite or full: two robots, one ecosystem

There are two versions of the hardware, aimed at two kinds of people - but they
share the exact same ecosystem.

<HtmlEmbed src="stack-two-versions.html" />

The only extra piece the lite needs is the **desktop app**, which runs the
robot's software on your computer. All you have to do is connect to the robot in
the desktop app and sign in with your Hugging Face account. From then on, as
long as the robot stays connected to the desktop app, it shows up in the mobile
app and joins the exact same ecosystem as a wireless one.

## The app store

### One store, open to everyone

Reachy Mini has an app store, and you reach it right from the mobile app.
Browse the list, tap an app, and it runs on your robot. There is nothing to
install and nothing to update. You can also [browse the apps](/reachy-mini/apps)
right here on the site to see what is already out there.

<Stack layout="2-column" gap="large">
<Video src="/videos/store.mp4" aspect="968 / 1700" maxWidth={300}>The app store, right inside the mobile app: browse the list, tap an app, and it runs on your robot right away.</Video>
</Stack>

Every app in the store is a **Hugging Face Space**: a static web page hosted for
free on Hugging Face. Publishing is open to everyone - push a Space, tag it with
`reachy_mini_js_app`, and it shows up in the store like any other, right next to
the official ones.

<Note variant="info" emoji="🛡️" title="Open, but not a free-for-all">
A featured list highlights trusted apps, a blocklist removes bad ones, and an
automatic filter screens content before it reaches users. Publishing stays
easy; the store stays safe to browse.
</Note>

### In the app, or in any browser

Because every app is just a web page, the same app can run in two places without
a single change to its code. Most of the time it runs **embedded** inside a
host - the mobile app, or the vibe coder's live preview. The host signs you in,
picks your robot and opens the connection once, then hands the running app a
robot that is already awake; the app itself never deals with login or setup.

The same app can also run **standalone in any browser**. A small host shell
turns a plain browser tab into a full Reachy Mini client: open a link, sign in
once, pick your robot, and the app drives it - no mobile app in the loop. Same
code either way, only the way you get in differs.

## Make your own, in plain words

Conversation and the store apps are only the start. When you want something that
does not exist yet, you describe it and an **AI agent** builds it - the
[Reachy Mini vibe coder](https://huggingface.co/spaces/tfrere/reachy-mini-vibe-coding-apps)
writes the app and runs it live on your robot. Or, if you would rather write the
code yourself, you take the wheel and build it locally.
Either way you end up with an ordinary web app that runs on your real robot and
lives in the same store as everyone else's.

## Wrapping up

Reachy Mini is a small robot, but the software around it is what makes it feel
open. You set it up with an app, you add to it from a store, and when you want
something that does not exist yet, you describe it and an agent builds it.

None of this asks you to be a roboticist. The app hides the setup, the SDK
hides the robotics, and the store hides the deployment. What is left is
deciding what your robot should do.

<Note variant="info" emoji="🤖" title="The takeaway">
An open robot is only as open as the software around it. Reachy Mini's software
is built so that using it, and building for it, are things anyone can do.
</Note>

If you have a Reachy Mini, or just an idea for what one should do, the whole
galaxy is already open to you.
